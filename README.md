RAG-Based Medical Chatbot: Retrieval Optimization for Clinical Support
This project presents an in-depth research on the implementation of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture,specifically designed to mitigate hallucination risks in Large Language Models (LLMs) within the medical domain. The primary focus of this study is to analyze how technical parameters, particularly chunk size variations and embedding model selection, directly impact the quality of medical information provided to patients. Through rigorous comparative experiments using the RAGAS framework, the system evaluates the performance of BGE Small and MiniLM-L6 models in processing Clinical Practice Guidelines validated by authoritative health organizations such as the WHO and the Indonesian Ministry of Health.

The research offers practical contributions by establishing an optimal technical configuration, where the combination of the BGE Small model and a 512-token chunk size proved to deliver the best balance between semantic accuracy and contextual completeness. These findings are integrated into an interactive chatbot prototype built with Streamlit and LangChain, capable of delivering reliable, factual, and verifiable medical responses. This project demonstrates that with proper retrieval optimization, AI technology can serve as a strategic solution for educating the public on self-management of minor illnesses without compromising medical information safety.
